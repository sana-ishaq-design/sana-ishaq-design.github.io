05 — The Audit Process

Five steps. Fixed timeline.
No ambiguity.

Every UX audit follows the same structured process, giving you complete visibility from kickoff to delivery. You'll know exactly what happens, what you'll receive, and how findings are prioritised—without scope creep or vague recommendations.

Every UX audit follows the same five-step process.

01
Product Review
Discovery call to understand your business goals, target users, and priority journeys. You'll provide access to your live product, staging environment, Figma files, and any available analytics data.
02
Usability Evaluation
End-to-end expert review of your product against Nielsen's 10 heuristics and Baymard Institute research. Every friction point documented, annotated, and severity-rated as Critical / High / Medium.
03
Analysis & Prioritisation
Findings ranked by user impact and conversion cost. Quick wins identified separately from structural issues. A prioritised action plan built for immediate implementation.
04
Audit Report Delivery
Full written report with annotated screenshots, severity ratings, prioritised recommendations, and a conversion analysis. Delivered as a structured PDF, Notion document, or Figma file depending on your team's workflow.
05
Walkthrough Session (60 min)
A live 60-minute walkthrough of every finding, recommendation, and priority. Your team can ask questions, discuss implementation approaches, and gain clarity on next steps. The session is recorded and shared for future reference.

A UX audit is a structured expert review of your product, website, or application measured against established usability principles, including Nielsen's 10 Heuristics and industry best practices. Every key screen and user flow is evaluated to uncover usability issues, conversion blockers, and opportunities for improvement. If you know something is affecting conversions, engagement, or retention but aren't sure what's causing it, a UX audit provides clear answers and prioritised recommendations without months of trial and error.
